FAQs about MusicMastering

What is Mastering?

Mastering is the last phase of audio production. It is the process of giving mixed music the finishing touches, improving its overall sound; the ‘Icing on the cake’ as it were! Mastering is critical as it makes the difference between a song sounding amateurish and one sounding professionally complete. Our mastering software employs effects like EQ, compression, limiting, saturation, and stereo imaging, similar to those used by leading online mastering services.

Is it Free?

Yes! Unlike other companies, who charge up to £50 per individual mastered song, we offer our mastering service free of charge to all our subscribers.

How does your Mastering service work?

Our software identifies the key audio characteristics of your chosen reference song and reproduces these on the song you wish to perfect. This includes elements like EQ, RMS, frequency response, peak amplitude, compression, and stereo width.

How should I prepare my song for mastering?

Ensure your mix is clear, with well-balanced instrumental and vocal levels, free from distortion and noise. Avoid excessive use of limiters and compression; our software will handle these aspects. Your song should be in high-quality MP3 or WAV format.

How to choose a Reference song?

The reference song should be of high quality, professionally recorded, mixed, and mastered, and of a similar genre and style to your song to ensure the best possible mastering outcome.

What happens to my uploaded song?
